Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

 Account & Password Safety
☐ Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
☐ Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, banking, and social media accounts.
☐ Never share OTPs, passwords, or login links—even with someone claiming to be support staff.

 Device & App Safety
☐ Download apps only from trusted sources (Google Play Store / Apple App Store).
☐ Keep your phone and laptop updated with the latest security patches.
☐ Avoid connecting to public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities (banking, payments).

 Phishing & Scams Awareness
☐ Don’t click on unknown links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages.
☐ Double-check sender details—scammers often mimic banks or popular apps.
☐ Be cautious of “too good to be true” offers, job scams, or urgent messages.

 UPI & Financial Safety (Important)
☐ Never approve a “Collect Request” unless you are expecting money—receiving money does NOT require approval.
☐ Always verify the UPI ID or QR code before sending money.
☐ Do not share screen or allow remote access apps (like AnyDesk/TeamViewer) during financial transactions.
☐ Set a daily transaction limit and enable app lock for UPI apps.

 Social Media Safety
☐ Keep your profiles private and limit personal information (phone number, address, college ID).
☐ Don’t accept friend requests from unknown people.
☐ Think before posting—content can be misused or stay online permanently.

 What to Do If Something Goes Wrong
☐ Immediately report fraud or suspicious activity at the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
☐ Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud—act fast!)
☐ Inform your bank or payment app support right away to block transactions.
